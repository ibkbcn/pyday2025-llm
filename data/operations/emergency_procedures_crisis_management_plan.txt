CRISIS MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK:

This document outlines comprehensive emergency procedures and crisis management protocols for Coffee Shop Empire's global operations, ensuring the safety of our employees, customers, and business continuity during emergency situations.

CRISIS MANAGEMENT TEAM STRUCTURE:

EXECUTIVE CRISIS TEAM:
Primary Crisis Team (Activated for Level 3+ incidents):
- Chief Executive Officer (Team Leader)
- Chief Operating Officer (Operations Response)
- Chief Financial Officer (Financial Impact)
- Chief Marketing Officer (Communications)
- Chief Human Resources Officer (Employee Care)
- Chief Legal Officer (Legal Compliance)
- Chief Technology Officer (IT/Systems)

Secondary Crisis Team (Activated for Level 2+ incidents):
- Regional Operations Directors
- Communications Director
- Risk Management Director
- Security Director
- Facilities Director
- Supply Chain Director

Local Response Teams (Store Level):
- Store Manager (Incident Commander)
- Assistant Manager (Employee Coordinator)
- Shift Supervisor (Customer Safety)
- Designated Safety Officers

CRISIS CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM:

LEVEL 1 - MINOR INCIDENT:
- Single store impact
- Minimal business disruption (<4 hours)
- No serious injuries
- Local response only
- Examples: Minor equipment failure, local power outage

LEVEL 2 - MODERATE INCIDENT:
- Multiple stores or significant single-store impact
- Business disruption (4-24 hours)
- Minor injuries possible
- Regional response required
- Examples: Regional power outage, severe weather event

LEVEL 3 - MAJOR INCIDENT:
- Significant regional impact or national crisis
- Extended business disruption (1-7 days)
- Serious injuries possible
- Executive team activation
- Examples: Natural disasters, product recalls, security incidents

LEVEL 4 - CATASTROPHIC INCIDENT:
- National or international impact
- Extended business disruption (>7 days)
- Multiple serious injuries or fatalities
- Full crisis team activation
- Examples: Terrorism, pandemic, major supply chain collapse

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES BY INCIDENT TYPE:

MEDICAL EMERGENCIES:

Life-Threatening Medical Emergency:
1. Immediately call emergency services (911/local equivalent)
2. Store Manager takes charge of scene
3. Designate employee to meet emergency responders
4. Clear area around patient, provide first aid if trained
5. Document incident details and witness information
6. Contact Risk Management Department within 1 hour
7. Preserve security footage for investigation

Non-Life-Threatening Medical Emergency:
1. Assess situation and provide basic first aid
2. Offer to call emergency services if needed
3. Document incident and customer information
4. Complete incident report within 24 hours
5. Follow up with affected individual

COVID-19/Communicable Disease Response:
1. Isolate symptomatic individual immediately
2. Follow local health department guidelines
3. Deep clean affected area
4. Notify close contacts per health guidelines
5. Implement enhanced cleaning protocols
6. Consider temporary closure if required

FIRE EMERGENCIES:

Fire Alarm Activation:
1. Evacuate all employees and customers immediately
2. Designate meeting point 200 feet from building
3. Account for all employees
4. Call fire department if not automatically notified
5. Do not re-enter building until cleared by authorities
6. Contact District Manager and Risk Management

Small Controllable Fire:
1. Evacuate immediate area
2. Use appropriate fire extinguisher if trained
3. Pull fire alarm to alert others
4. Call fire department
5. Evacuate building if fire cannot be controlled
6. Document incident and complete report

WEATHER-RELATED EMERGENCIES:

Severe Weather Warning:
1. Monitor weather alerts continuously
2. Prepare emergency supplies (flashlights, water, first aid)
3. Secure outdoor furniture and equipment
4. Backup critical data and systems
5. Communicate with employees about safety procedures
6. Consider early closure if safety risks increase

Tornado/Severe Storm:
1. Move customers and staff to designated safe areas
2. Stay away from windows and glass doors
3. Monitor weather radio for updates
4. Account for all individuals
5. Wait for all-clear from authorities
6. Document damages and contact insurance

Hurricane/Typhoon Preparation:
1. Board up windows and doors
2. Secure outdoor equipment
3. Stock emergency supplies (72-hour kit)
4. Backup critical business data
5. Communicate closure plans to customers
6. Evacuate if ordered by authorities

SECURITY INCIDENTS:

Robbery/Theft:
1. Cooperate with perpetrator demands
2. Do not resist or escalate situation
3. Observe and remember perpetrator details
4. Call police after perpetrator leaves
5. Secure crime scene, preserve evidence
6. Contact District Manager immediately
7. Provide counseling support for affected employees

Active Threat/Shooting:
1. RUN: Evacuate if safe to do so
2. HIDE: Lock doors, silence phones, stay quiet
3. FIGHT: As last resort, defend yourself
4. Call 911 when safe
5. Follow law enforcement instructions
6. Account for all employees and customers
7. Provide trauma counseling

Suspicious Package/Threat:
1. Do not touch or move suspicious item
2. Evacuate immediate area
3. Call security or law enforcement
4. Follow professional bomb squad instructions
5. Document information and witnesses
6. Coordinate with local authorities

UTILITY FAILURES:

Power Outage:
1. Assess safety conditions (lighting, equipment)
2. Use emergency lighting if available
3. Consider temporary closure if unsafe
4. Contact utility company for ETA on restoration
5. Preserve perishable food items
6. Communicate with customers about status

Water Main Break:
1. Close water valves if safe to do so
2. Discontinue food service if water quality affected
3. Contact utility company immediately
4. Consider temporary closure
5. Document damages and business impact
6. File insurance claim if applicable

Gas Leak:
1. Evacuate building immediately
2. Do not use electrical devices or phones
3. Call gas company from safe location
4. Do not re-enter until cleared by professionals
5. Ventilate building if safe
6. Document incident for insurance

SUPPLY CHAIN DISRUPTIONS:

Product Recall:
1. Immediately remove recalled products from shelves
2. Stop serving affected items
3. Contact all affected locations
4. Document inventory quantities
5. Coordinate with supplier for return/replacement
6. Communicate with customers if needed
7. Complete regulatory reporting requirements

Delivery Disruption:
1. Activate backup suppliers if available
2. Modify menu based on available ingredients
3. Communicate temporary changes to customers
4. Document financial impact
5. Coordinate with supply chain team
6. Implement contingency procedures

TECHNOLOGY FAILURES:

POS System Failure:
1. Switch to manual backup procedures
2. Use calculators and handwritten receipts
3. Contact IT support immediately
4. Preserve cash security procedures
5. Document transactions for later entry
6. Consider closure if system critical for operations

Cybersecurity Incident:
1. Immediately disconnect affected systems
2. Contact IT Security Team
3. Do not attempt to fix systems independently
4. Preserve system logs and evidence
5. Follow data breach notification procedures
6. Document timeline and impact

FOOD SAFETY EMERGENCIES:

Foodborne Illness Outbreak:
1. Immediately stop serving suspect food items
2. Preserve samples for testing
3. Document affected customers and symptoms
4. Contact local health department
5. Cooperate with investigation
6. Implement enhanced cleaning protocols
7. Provide medical information to affected customers

Allergic Reaction Emergency:
1. Call emergency services immediately
3. Preserve food sample for allergen testing
4. Document ingredients and preparation methods
5. Complete incident report
6. Review allergen protocols with staff

NATURAL DISASTERS:

Earthquake:
1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On during shaking
2. Evacuate building after shaking stops
3. Check for injuries and provide first aid
4. Assess building damage
5. Account for all employees and customers
6. Contact emergency services if needed
7. Do not re-enter until structural safety confirmed

Flood:
1. Evacuate area before flooding if possible
2. Move to higher ground
3. Turn off utilities if safe to do so
4. Avoid walking/biking in flood waters
5. Document damages for insurance
6. Coordinate with emergency services
7. Implement mold prevention after water recedes

BUSINESS CONTINUITY PROCEDURES:

IMMEDIATE RESPONSE (First 4 Hours):
1. Activate appropriate crisis team
2. Assess situation and impact
3. Ensure employee and customer safety
4. Secure property and assets
5. Initiate emergency communications
6. Document incident details

SHORT-TERM RESPONSE (First 24 Hours):
1. Implement business continuity plan
2. Coordinate with emergency services
3. Begin insurance claim process
4. Communicate with stakeholders
5. Activate employee assistance programs
6. Monitor media coverage

MEDIUM-TERM RECOVERY (First Week):
1. Damage assessment and repair
2. Temporary relocation if necessary
3. Employee support and counseling
4. Customer communications and compensation
5. Supply chain restoration
6. Review and improve procedures

LONG-TERM RECOVERY (First Month):
1. Complete repairs and restoration
2. Review insurance claims
3. Update emergency procedures
4. Conduct employee debriefing
5. Implement improvements
6. Document lessons learned
