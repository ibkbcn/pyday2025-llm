INNOVATION PROCESS AND GOVERNANCE:

PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT STAGES:
Stage 1: Idea Generation (Continuous)
- Customer feedback analysis
- Trend monitoring
- Employee suggestion program
- Competitive analysis
- Market research

Stage 2: Concept Development (Monthly review)
- Feasibility studies
- Cost analysis
- Market testing
- Resource planning
- Risk assessment

Stage 3: Recipe Testing (Quarterly cycles)
- Kitchen development
- Taste panel evaluations
- Nutritional analysis
- Cost optimization
- Shelf-life testing

Stage 4: Market Testing (Selected locations)
- Pilot store testing
- Customer acceptance studies
- Sales performance analysis
- Operational feasibility
- Supply chain validation

Stage 5: Full Launch (After successful testing)
- Global rollout planning
- Marketing campaign development
- Staff training programs
- Supply chain scaling
- Performance monitoring

DECISION-MAKING CRITERIA:
Financial Metrics:
- ROI > 25% within 2 years
- Profit margin > 60%
- Payback period < 18 months
- Investment capacity within budget

Customer Metrics:
- Target market size > 50,000 customers
- Purchase intent > 70%
- Satisfaction score > 4.5/5.0
- Repeat purchase rate > 40%

Operational Metrics:
- Implementation time < 6 months
- Staff training requirements < 4 hours
- Equipment investment < $50,000 per store
- Supply chain readiness < 90 days

Strategic Alignment:
- Brand fit assessment
- Sustainability impact
- Innovation leadership potential
- Competitive advantage creation

RISK MANAGEMENT:

INNOVATION RISKS:
- Market acceptance failure: Mitigated by extensive testing
- Supply chain disruption: Mitigated by multiple suppliers
- Operational complexity: Mitigated by simplified procedures
- Cannibalization of existing products: Monitored and managed
- Regulatory compliance: Legal review at each stage

MITIGATION STRATEGIES:
- Phased rollout approach
- Pilot testing before global launch
- Supplier diversification
- Comprehensive staff training
- Continuous performance monitoring
